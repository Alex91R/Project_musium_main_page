What you will find inside

The Kyiv War Museum in Ukraine is dedicated to commemorating and exploring the history of war and its impact on the country â€” particularly the period of the Second World War and recent conflicts. It serves as both a memorial complex and a museum of history. The museum's mission is to preserve the memory of those who suffered and those who defended Ukraine, to document and interpret war experiences, and to engage visitors in reflection about war, resilience, and heritage.

The National Museum of the History of Ukraine in the Second World War is one of the largest museums in the country, dedicated to preserving the memory of war and its impact on the Ukrainian people. Its main goal is to honor the courage and suffering of those who fought for freedom and to remind visitors of the true cost of peace. The museum tells the story not only of the Second World War but also of modern military conflicts in which Ukraine continues to defend its independence.

It the best choice if you want to spend time interesting. The museum is both educational, documenting history, showing artifacts and engaging with current events and public memory.

The dominant feature of the entire complex is the tallest monument in Europe, 102 meters high, "Motherland", the pedestal of which houses the museum's Main Building.
